Travel Tips

Everything you need to know before your trip to Japan

Practical Information

Travelling to Japan for the first time can feel a bit overwhelming but once you know the basics it is actually a very easy and stress-free country to travel around.

Getting Around

  • Get a Suica or Pasmo card at any major train station — it works on almost all trains and buses
  • The Shinkansen bullet train connects all major cities — a Japan Rail Pass is worth buying if travelling between multiple cities
  • Taxis in Japan are expensive so try to avoid them for longer journeys
  • Cycling is a great way to get around smaller cities like Kyoto
  • Google Maps works very well in Japan for planning train journeys

Money and Payments

  • Japan is still largely a cash-based society so always carry yen with you
  • 7-Eleven ATMs accept most foreign bank cards
  • The currency is Japanese Yen (JPY)
  • Budget around 3,000 to 5,000 yen per day for food at local places
  • Credit card use is increasing in cities but smaller shops may be cash only

Language

  • English is not widely spoken outside tourist areas but most signs in cities have English
  • Download Google Translate with Japanese offline — the camera feature is very useful for menus
  • Learning a few phrases like "arigatou gozaimasu" (thank you) will be appreciated by locals
  • Most restaurants display plastic food models outside so you can point to what you want

Safety and Etiquette

  • Japan has an extremely low crime rate and is one of the safest countries for tourists
  • Do not speak on the phone on trains — this is considered very rude
  • Always queue properly and wait for people to exit before boarding
  • Do not eat or drink while walking
  • Rubbish bins are rare in public — carry a small bag for your litter

Best Time to Visit

When to Go

Japan is beautiful at any time of year but the two most popular seasons are spring and autumn.

Spring (March to May) is cherry blossom season. Parks and streets are covered in pink and white blossoms. This is the busiest and most expensive period to travel.

Autumn (October to November) brings beautiful red and orange foliage, particularly stunning around temples and forests.

Summer (June to August) can be very hot and humid but it is festival season. Winter is cold but great for skiing and visiting hot spring baths.

What to Pack: Comfortable walking shoes are essential as you will walk a lot. A portable Wi-Fi device or SIM card is highly recommended. Bring a small backpack for day trips and a light jacket for cooler evenings.
