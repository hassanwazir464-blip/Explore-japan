Travel Tips

Everything you need to know before your trip to Japan

Practical Information

Travelling to Japan for the first time can feel a bit overwhelming because things are done very differently to what most Western tourists are used to. However, once you know the basics Japan is actually a very easy and stress-free country to travel around. Here are some tips to help you prepare.

Getting Around

  • Get a Suica or Pasmo card (rechargeable travel card) at any major train station – it works on almost all trains and buses
  • The Shinkansen (bullet train) connects all major cities and is very fast – a Japan Rail Pass is worth buying if you are travelling between multiple cities
  • Taxis in Japan are expensive, so try to avoid them for longer journeys
  • Cycling is a great way to get around smaller cities like Kyoto – bikes can be hired cheaply near most train stations
  • Google Maps works very well in Japan for planning train journeys

Money and Payments

  • Japan is still largely a cash-based society so always carry yen with you
  • 7-Eleven ATMs accept most foreign bank cards and are available across the country
  • The currency is Japanese Yen (JPY) – at the time of writing, £1 is roughly 190 yen
  • Budget around 3,000 to 5,000 yen per day for food if eating mostly at local places
  • Credit card use is increasing, especially in cities, but smaller shops and temples may be cash only

Language

  • English is not widely spoken outside of tourist areas, but most signs in cities have English translations
  • Download Google Translate with Japanese offline – the camera translation feature is very useful for menus and signs
  • Learning a few basic Japanese phrases such as "arigatou gozaimasu" (thank you) and "sumimasen" (excuse me) will be much appreciated by locals
  • Most restaurants display plastic models of their food outside so you can point to what you want even without speaking Japanese

Safety and Etiquette

  • Japan has an extremely low crime rate and is one of the safest countries for tourists in the world
  • Do not speak on the phone on trains – this is considered very rude. Keep noise to a minimum
  • Always queue properly and wait for people to exit the train before boarding
  • Do not eat or drink while walking – find somewhere to sit down first
  • Rubbish bins are very rare in public – carry a small bag to hold your litter until you find one

Best Time to Visit

When to Go

Japan is a beautiful destination at any time of year, but the two most popular seasons are spring and autumn.

Spring (March to May) is cherry blossom season, which is perhaps the most iconic time to visit Japan. Parks and streets are covered in pink and white blossoms and locals celebrate with picnics beneath the trees (called "hanami"). This is the busiest and most expensive period to travel.

Autumn (October to November) brings beautiful red and orange foliage, particularly stunning around temples and forests. Temperatures are pleasant and crowds are slightly smaller than spring.

Summer (June to August) can be very hot and humid, but it is festival season with many vibrant events taking place. Winter is cold but great for skiing in the northern regions and visiting onsen (hot spring baths).

What to Pack: Comfortable walking shoes are essential as you will walk a lot. A portable Wi-Fi device or SIM card is highly recommended. Bring a small backpack for day trips. A light jacket is useful even in summer for air-conditioned buildings and cooler evenings.
