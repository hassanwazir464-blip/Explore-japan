Destinations

Explore the most incredible places Japan has to offer

Top Places to Visit

Japan has so many amazing places to explore. Below are four of the most popular destinations for tourists. Each one offers a completely different experience, so many visitors try to include more than one on their trip.

Tokyo

Tokyo is the capital of Japan and one of the largest cities in the world. It is a place where ultra-modern skyscrapers sit alongside traditional shrines and temples. The city is divided into many different districts, each with its own personality.

Shibuya is famous for its busy crossing, while Akihabara is known as the electronics and anime district. Harajuku is popular with young people for its unique fashion, and Shinjuku has some of the best nightlife in the city.

Tokyo is also incredibly easy to get around thanks to its extensive and punctual metro system. Most signs are in English as well as Japanese.

Kyoto

Kyoto was the capital of Japan for over 1,000 years and it remains the cultural and spiritual centre of the country. It is home to over 1,600 temples and shrines, including the famous Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) and Fushimi Inari with its thousands of orange torii gates.

Kyoto is also one of the best places in Japan to spot geishas, especially in the historic Gion district. The city is particularly beautiful in spring during cherry blossom season when the parks and temple gardens are covered in pink flowers.

Kyoto is about 2.5 hours from Tokyo by Shinkansen (bullet train) and is easy to combine with a visit to Nara or Osaka.

Osaka

Osaka is Japan's third largest city and is famous for being the food capital of the country. The locals have a saying: "kuidaore" which roughly means "eat until you drop". The Dotonbori district is a must-visit, packed with street food stalls, neon signs, and restaurants.

Popular foods to try in Osaka include takoyaki (octopus balls), okonomiyaki (savoury pancakes), and kushikatsu (deep fried skewers). Osaka also has its own castle, the impressive Osaka Castle, which is set in a large park.

Osaka is very close to Kyoto (just 15 minutes by train) so many tourists visit both cities on the same trip.

Hiroshima

Hiroshima is one of the most historically important cities in Japan. It is known worldwide as the site of the first atomic bomb attack in 1945. Today the city is a symbol of peace and recovery, and the Peace Memorial Park and Museum are deeply moving places to visit.

Despite its history, Hiroshima is a vibrant, modern city with a great food scene. It also has its own version of okonomiyaki, which locals are very proud of.

Nearby Miyajima Island is one of the most photographed places in Japan, featuring a giant torii gate that appears to float on the water at high tide.
