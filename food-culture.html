Food & Culture

Experience the flavours and traditions of Japan

Japanese Food

Japanese cuisine is known all over the world for its quality, variety, and presentation. Japan has more Michelin-starred restaurants than any other country in the world.

Sushi and Sashimi

Sushi is probably the most well-known Japanese food outside of Japan. It consists of vinegared rice topped with fresh fish, seafood, or vegetables. Sashimi is similar but without the rice.

In Japan you can eat sushi at a wide range of places, from high-end restaurants to affordable kaiten-zushi (conveyor belt sushi) restaurants where plates pass by on a moving belt and you take what you want.

Ramen

Ramen is a type of Japanese noodle soup that has become popular worldwide. There are many different regional styles of ramen in Japan, each with its own unique broth and toppings. The four main types are tonkotsu, shoyu, miso, and shio.

Ramen shops can be found on almost every street in Japan and a decent bowl typically costs between 800 and 1500 yen.

Other Foods to Try

Tempura

Lightly battered and deep fried vegetables or seafood, served with a dipping sauce. Simple but absolutely delicious.

Matcha

Finely ground green tea powder used in drinks, ice cream, and sweets. A uniquely Japanese flavour that is very popular with tourists.

Yakitori

Grilled chicken skewers cooked over charcoal. Found at festivals and small grill restaurants called izakayas.

Japanese Culture

Japan has a rich and unique culture. Understanding some basic customs will help you get the most out of your visit.

Temples and Shrines

Japan has two main types of religious sites: Shinto shrines and Buddhist temples. Shrines are marked by a torii gate at the entrance. Temples are Buddhist and often have a pagoda and incense burners.

When visiting these places it is important to be respectful and quiet. Dress modestly and avoid loud behaviour.

Cultural Tips: In Japan it is considered rude to eat or drink while walking. You should also remove your shoes before entering someone's home or certain traditional restaurants. Tipping is not part of Japanese culture.
